## 📌 Executive Overview

This project analyzes historical maternity ward data from Vienna General
Hospital (1841--1849) to evaluate one of the most transformative medical
interventions in modern history: mandatory handwashing.

At a time when disease was attributed to "bad air" or invisible forces,
Dr. Ignaz Semmelweis relied on structured observation and measurable
evidence. His hypothesis was simple but bold --- physician hand hygiene
could drastically reduce maternal mortality.

This project reconstructs that investigation using modern data
science tools.

Clear data. Clear reasoning. Clear conclusions.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 🎯 Objectives

-   Perform structured exploratory data analysis
-   Quantify maternal mortality rates
-   Compare pre‑ and post‑intervention outcomes
-   Visualize distribution shifts using histograms and KDE
-   Conduct statistical hypothesis testing (independent t-test)
-   Build a rigorous, data-driven argument

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 📊 Key Findings

-   Maternal mortality dropped significantly after the introduction of
    chlorinated handwashing in June 1847.
-   The reduction is statistically significant (p-value \< 0.01).
-   The probability of death during childbirth decreased multiple times
    after policy enforcement.

The data leaves little room for doubt: hand hygiene saved lives.

Sometimes progress does not require complexity --- just discipline and
evidence.
