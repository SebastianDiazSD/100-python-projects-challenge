chars_to_remove = [',', '$']
columns_to_clean = ['USD_Production_Budget',

for col in columns_to_clean:

🕰️ Converting Years to Decades – A Clean Data Trick¶

Time to organize the films historically.

Instead of analyzing individual years, let's group movies by decade. This helps us see broader trends.

Here’s the plan:

  1. Convert Release_Date into a DatetimeIndex
  2. Extract the year
  3. Use floor division (//) to convert years into decades
  4. Add a new column called Decade

Simple. Elegant. Effective.

In [17]:
# Create a DatetimeIndex from Release_Date
dt_index = pd.DatetimeIndex(df_clean.Release_Date)

# Extract years
years = dt_index.year

years[:5]
Out[17]:
Index([1915, 1916, 1916, 1920, 1925], dtype='int32', name='Release_Date')

Now let’s understand the decade conversion logic.

In [18]:
# Example: How does 1999 become 1990?

1999 // 10
Out[18]:
199
In [19]:
1999 // 10 * 10
Out[19]:
1990

That’s the trick. Floor division removes the last digit. Then multiplying by 10 rebuilds the decade.

Now we apply it to the full dataset.

In [20]:
# Convert years to decades
decades = years // 10 * 10

# Add new column to the DataFrame
df_clean['Decade'] = decades

print("New Era Intercept:", model_new.intercept_)
