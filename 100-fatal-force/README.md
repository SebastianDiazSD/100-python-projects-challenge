## Overview

**Fatal Force** is a data analysis project examining fatal police
shootings in the United States using the Washington Post database
combined with U.S. Census socioeconomic indicators.

The analysis explores patterns related to:

-   Poverty levels
-   Median household income
-   Educational attainment
-   Racial demographics
-   Geographic distribution of incidents

The objective is to provide **data-driven insights into structural and
social patterns associated with fatal police use of force**.

This work forms part of the **100 Python Projects portfolio**,
demonstrating applied data analysis, statistical exploration, and data
visualization using Python.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Data Sources

This project integrates multiple public datasets:

  
  |Dataset|                             Description|
  ---|---|
  |Washington Post Fatal Force Database|         Fatal police shootings in the U.S. since 2015|
  |US Census Poverty Data|              Percentage of people below the poverty line|
  |US Census Education Data|            High school completion rates|
  |US Census Income Data|               Median household income|
  |City Racial Demographics|            Population racial composition by city|

## Key Questions

The analysis investigates several important questions:

-   How have fatal police shootings evolved over time?
-   What demographic groups are most affected?
-   Are there geographic patterns in the distribution of incidents?
-   How do socioeconomic indicators correlate with fatal police
    encounters?
-   Do structural inequalities appear in the geographic distribution of
    fatal force?

## Analytical Highlights

The notebook includes:

-   Socioeconomic comparisons
